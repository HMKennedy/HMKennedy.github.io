A Little About Me

Hi there! I’m Heather Kennedy a UX Designer living in the Phoenix area.

I attended the College of Charleston, where I graduated summa cum laude with a BA in Historic Preservation and Community Planning. As an architectural historian, I meticulously researched the history of historic buildings and documented their architectural attributes. In this field, I saw that a building designed one hundred years ago could still be relevant and useful today, and I learned that good design should be adaptable.

After moving to Phoenix, I entered the library field where I could embrace my love of literature and explore graphic design. I was able to give free rein to my creative side designing library posters and brochures. During a library website redesign project, I discovered the UX/UI field. The combination of research and design seemed ideally suited to my talents and interests, and I decided to make a career change.

An excellent collaborator, I believe design is not a solitary process. It requires a back and forth dialog between designer, users, and client to achieve the perfect balance between aesthetics and functionality.

In my spare time I enjoy reading, watching classic movies, hiking, and building Legos with my son. If you'd like to know more, please contact me.
